Investigation Overview¶

The overall goal of this presentation is to show the extant market segments within the greater San Francisco Bay area. These could be used to guide marketing efforts by identifying common user demographics, most frequent service use scenrios, and potential geographic hotspots.

This could also be used to guide marketing efforts away from smaller market segments which may not provide an optimal return on investment.

Dataset Overview and Executive Summary¶

This dataset includes information about individual rides made in a bike-sharing system from the greater San Francisco Bay area.

This is not a very diverse dataset in terms of variables. Instead, there is a large quantity of data across these variables which is great for answering our question of whether or not there are market segments that we can target.

We found that:

  1. The 'male-subscriber' market segment makes up a the vast majority of the user base, with about 3 times the second largest segment. That segment is the 'female-subscriber' group, which is itself about 3 times larger than the third largest group.

  2. While most users are born after 1980, there is also a sizeable (about 35,000 trips), active market segment born between 1960 and 1980 that could be targeted as a segment by itself.

  3. There are cases of long-duration usage (over 60 minutes) but according to the dataset anything over 40 minutes is an outlier so this is probably not a market segment worth targeting.

We also know that there is an active market between 1960 and 1980 that, while it might not be worth growing, can be quite profitable for a while.

This also gives us an indication of how users from a particular decade will decline as time passes, and what replacement rate we will need to maintain the business.

It also shows us that trips from subscriber users are about one third shorter than trips from casual customers. This is a case where it's very important to do some further customer reasearch and find out why this is. This information could lead to better targeting to grow these user segments separately.

This chart also shows that the market segment for trips above 40 minutes is either under-utilized or non-existant. Again, more research would be required to discover which one of these is true.
